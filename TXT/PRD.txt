1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RealmForge is a web-based platform for playing Dungeons & Dragons 5th
Edition online. It removes the most common barrier to playing D&D — the need for
a human Dungeon Master — by replacing that role with a large language model
(Groq Llama 3.3 70B) that narrates the story, enforces rules, and drives the
world in real time.

The platform supports two modes: fully autonomous AI-DM campaigns where the AI
handles everything, and human-DM campaigns where a person runs the game with an
AI copilot (Claude Haiku) available for suggestions. Both modes support
multiplayer with real-time synchronization across all players.

The core value proposition is: pick up and play a D&D campaign with friends,
any time, without scheduling around a DM.

2. PROBLEM STATEMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

2.1 The DM Bottleneck
  - Finding and retaining a Dungeon Master is the #1 reason groups don't play.
  - A DM must prepare hours of content per session, adjudicate rules live, and
    improvise narrative — a high-skill, high-time commitment role.
  - Most groups play infrequently or not at all because no one wants the job.

2.2 Existing Alternatives Are Incomplete
  - Virtual tabletops (Roll20, Foundry) require a human DM and focus on maps
    and dice, not storytelling.
  - AI chatbots (ChatGPT, Claude.ai) can improv a story but have no persistent
    world state, no rules enforcement, no multiplayer, and no character system.
  - No existing product combines: AI narration + full D&D 5e rules + real-time
    multiplayer + persistent campaign state.

2.3 Target Users
  Primary:  Friend groups who want to play D&D but lack a willing DM.
  Secondary: Experienced DMs who want AI assistance to reduce prep work.
  Tertiary: Solo players who want a D&D experience without a group.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3. GOALS AND SUCCESS METRICS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

3.1 Product Goals
  G1. Let a group of friends start and finish a D&D session with zero prep.
  G2. Make AI narration feel like a skilled human DM — reactive, consistent,
      and narratively satisfying.
  G3. Enforce D&D 5e rules accurately enough that the game feels fair.
  G4. Support real-time multiplayer so all players are always in sync.
  G5. Keep AI costs low enough for sustainable operation.

3.2 Success Metrics
  - Session completion rate: % of campaigns that reach a natural stopping point
    rather than being abandoned.
  - Narration quality score: qualitative user rating of AI narration per session.
  - Rules error rate: frequency of incorrect mechanical outcomes (damage, saves).
  - Time-to-first-action: time from account creation to first player action sent.
  - Cost-per-session: total Groq/Anthropic API spend per completed session.

4. USER PERSONAS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PERSONA A — "The Group That Never Plays"
  Age: 22–35. Has a friend group that loves D&D but hasn't played in months.
  Pain: No one has time to DM. Sessions get cancelled.
  Goal: Drop into a campaign on a Friday night with minimal setup.
  Tech comfort: Medium. Uses web apps daily, not developers.

PERSONA B — "The Busy DM"
  Age: 25–40. Experienced DM, runs games occasionally.
  Pain: Prep takes 5–10 hours per session. AI could do the filler content.
  Goal: Use the AI copilot to speed up their own campaigns.
  Tech comfort: High. Has used Roll20 or Foundry before.

PERSONA C — "The Solo Explorer"
  Age: 18–30. Wants the D&D experience but has no group.
  Pain: D&D requires 3+ people. Solo play is underserved.
  Goal: Run a solo campaign with the AI as DM and narrator.
  Tech comfort: Medium-high.

5. FEATURES AND REQUIREMENTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

5.1 Authentication and User Management

  REQ-AUTH-1   Users can register with email and password.
  REQ-AUTH-2   Users can log in and receive a persistent session.
  REQ-AUTH-3   Protected routes (/dashboard, /campaign, /create-campaign)
               redirect unauthenticated users to /login.
  REQ-AUTH-4   Logged-in users are redirected away from /login and /register.
  REQ-AUTH-5   All database queries are scoped to the authenticated user via
               Row Level Security — users cannot read or modify other users'
               data.

5.2 Campaign Management

  REQ-CAMP-1   A user can create a campaign with: name, setting description,
               and DM mode (AI or Human).
  REQ-CAMP-2   Campaign creation automatically generates a unique 8-character
               invite code and displays it to the user to let them share it
               with their friends.
  REQ-CAMP-3   Any authenticated user can join a campaign by entering a valid
               invite code.
  REQ-CAMP-4   The dashboard lists all campaigns the user is a member of.
  REQ-CAMP-5   The campaign creator is designated as DM and has access to the
               DM Console in Human mode.
  REQ-CAMP-6   Campaign state (current scene, world state) persists across
               sessions for that particular campaign only.
  REQ-CAMP-7  Allow the user to archive the campaign whenever they choose to.

5.3 Character Creation and Management

  REQ-CHAR-1   Players can create one character per campaign.
  REQ-CHAR-2   Character creation includes: name, race, class, subclass,
               and backstory.
  REQ-CHAR-3   Ability scores are determined by rolling 4d6 and dropping the
               lowest die, six times.
  REQ-CHAR-4   All D&D 5e standard races and classes are available.
  REQ-CHAR-5   The system calculates derived stats automatically: ability
               modifiers, skill bonuses, saving throw bonuses, AC, and
               proficiency bonus.
  REQ-CHAR-6   Characters persist: HP, XP, conditions, inventory, spells,
               and death saves are stored and synced in real time.
  REQ-CHAR-7   The character panel shows: HP bar, XP bar, AC, conditions,
               and a button to open the full character sheet.
  REQ-CHAR-8   The full character sheet shows all stats across tabs:
               Overview, Spells, Inventory.

5.4 AI Dungeon Master (AI Mode)

  REQ-AIDM-1   When a player submits an action, the AI DM generates a
               narrative response of 1–2 paragraphs.
  REQ-AIDM-2   The DM system prompt is built dynamically and includes:
               campaign setting, character summaries, active NPCs, active
               quests, current scene, and recent world memory summaries.
  REQ-AIDM-3   The AI must embed structured game events as XML tags within
               its narration. Supported event types:
                 - damage (target, amount, type)
                 - heal (target, amount)
                 - xp (amount)
                 - loot (item name, rarity)
                 - condition_add (target, condition)
                 - start_combat (monster names)
                 - new_npc (name, description)
                 - scene_update (description)
  REQ-AIDM-4   Parsed events must be applied atomically to the database
               before the narrative response is returned to the client.
  REQ-AIDM-5   To control token usage, only the last 6 messages are included
               in the AI prompt context window.
  REQ-AIDM-6   Every 20 narration messages, a 150–200 word session summary
               is generated (via Groq Llama 3.1 8B Instant) and stored.
               These summaries are injected into future prompts to maintain
               long-term continuity without overflowing the context window.
  REQ-AIDM-7   The AI model for narration is Groq Llama 3.3 70B.
               The AI model for summarization is Groq Llama 3.1 8B Instant.

5.5 Human DM Mode

  REQ-HRDM-1   In Human mode, the DM accesses a separate console page
               (/campaign/[id]/dm-console).
  REQ-HRDM-2   The DM console shows: the shared story log, a narration input,
               the AI copilot panel, a party overview, and a toolbox.
  REQ-HRDM-3   The DM types narration which is broadcast to all players
               and parsed for game events exactly as in AI mode.
  REQ-HRDM-4   The AI Copilot (Claude Haiku) generates suggested next lines
               for the DM based on campaign context and party state. The DM
               can accept a suggestion with one click.
  REQ-HRDM-5   The Party Overview shows all player characters' current HP,
               AC, level, and active conditions.
  REQ-HRDM-6   The DM Toolbox provides quick-reference tools and shortcuts.

5.6 Combat System

  REQ-CMBT-1   Combat begins when a start_combat event is parsed from the
               AI narration or when the DM manually initiates it.
  REQ-CMBT-2   On combat start, initiative is rolled for all combatants:
               1d20 + DEX modifier. Combatants are sorted descending by result.
  REQ-CMBT-3   The initiative tracker UI displays: turn order, current active
               combatant, HP/AC per combatant, active conditions, and round
               number.
  REQ-CMBT-4   On a player's turn they may: attack, use a skill, cast a spell,
               disengage, or take another action. Actions are resolved via
               the /api/combat/action endpoint.
  REQ-CMBT-5   Attack resolution: 1d20 + attack modifier vs. target AC.
               Natural 20 = critical hit (double damage dice).
               Natural 1 = automatic miss.
  REQ-CMBT-6   Damage is applied: current HP reduced, then temp HP if any.
  REQ-CMBT-7   Active conditions modify combat rolls as per D&D 5e rules
               (e.g., blinded imposes disadvantage on attack rolls).
  REQ-CMBT-8   Death saves: when HP reaches 0, the character makes a death
               saving throw (DC 10). Three successes = stabilized.
               Three failures = dead.
  REQ-CMBT-9   Monster turns are handled by the MonsterAI module.
               Basic monsters target the lowest-HP player and make a
               basic attack. Complex monsters with special abilities
               use Claude Haiku for decision-making.
  REQ-CMBT-10  Monster stat blocks are drawn from the monsters library.
               Damage immunities are respected.
  REQ-CMBT-11  Combat state (turn order, round, monster HP) is persisted in
               combat_encounters and synced to all clients via Realtime.

5.7 Dice System

  REQ-DICE-1   The platform supports all standard D&D dice: d4, d6, d8, d10,
               d12, d20, d100.
  REQ-DICE-2   Rolls can be made with advantage (roll twice, take higher)
               or disadvantage (roll twice, take lower).
  REQ-DICE-3   Skill checks, saving throws, and ability checks automatically
               apply the character's relevant modifier.
  REQ-DICE-4   The Dice Log sidebar shows the last 5 rolls, most recent first,
               with: die type, result, modifier, total, and purpose label.
  REQ-DICE-5   All dice rolls are recorded as messages in the messages table
               with their metadata.

5.8 Multiplayer and Real-Time Sync

  REQ-RT-1     All players in a campaign see narration, player actions, dice
               rolls, and combat log entries the moment they are created.
  REQ-RT-2     Character stat changes (HP, XP, conditions) propagate to all
               clients immediately via Supabase Realtime subscriptions.
  REQ-RT-3     Combat state changes (initiative updates, HP changes) are
               broadcast to all clients in real time.
  REQ-RT-4     The system does not use polling. All updates are event-driven.

5.9 World State and Persistence

  REQ-WORLD-1  NPCs encountered during play are stored with: name,
               description, disposition, location, and notes.
               They persist across sessions and are referenced in DM prompts.
  REQ-WORLD-2  Quests are tracked with: title, description, objectives,
               XP reward, and status (active / completed / abandoned).
  REQ-WORLD-3  The current scene description is stored on the campaign record
               and updated by scene_update events.
  REQ-WORLD-4  World state (a freeform JSONB field) can store additional
               campaign context for injection into the DM prompt.

5.10 XP and Progression

  REQ-XP-1     XP is awarded via xp game events parsed from narration.
  REQ-XP-2     The XP bar displays current XP and progress toward next level.
  REQ-XP-3     XP thresholds follow the D&D 5e standard level table.
  REQ-XP-4     On reaching a level threshold, the character's level increments.
               (Level-up UI wizard is a future requirement — see Section 7.)

6. NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  NFR-1  Security
         All tables enforce Row Level Security. No user can query, insert,
         update, or delete records outside their authorized scope.
         The Supabase service role key is never exposed to the client —
         used only in server-side API routes.

  NFR-2  Latency
         AI narration responses should complete within 5 seconds for typical
         prompts. The UI must show a loading/thinking state while waiting.

  NFR-3  Cost Control
         API costs are controlled by: limiting prompt history to last 6
         messages, using the 8B model for non-narrative tasks (summarization,
         simple monster AI), and using Claude Haiku (not Opus/Sonnet) for
         the DM copilot.

  NFR-4  Consistency
         The AI DM must maintain narrative consistency across a session.
         NPC names, quest details, and established world facts must not
         contradict themselves. World memory summaries are the mechanism
         for achieving this.

  NFR-5  Availability
         The platform depends on Supabase and Groq/Anthropic uptime.
         Errors from external AI APIs should be caught and surfaced as
         user-friendly messages rather than crashes.

  NFR-6  Accessibility
         Minimum: sufficient color contrast on all text, keyboard-navigable
         primary flows (login, send action, roll dice).

7. FUTURE REQUIREMENTS (OUT OF CURRENT SCOPE)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  FUTURE-1  Spell Casting System (Medium priority)
            Full spell resolution: slot deduction on cast, damage/healing
            effects applied as game events, concentration tracking,
            spell save DCs, area-of-effect resolution.

  FUTURE-2  Equipment and Item Mechanics (Medium priority)
            Equipping weapons and armor modifies AC and attack bonus.
            Consumables (potions) trigger heal events on use.
            Attunement slot limit enforcement.
            Item use as a combat action.

  FUTURE-3  Level-Up UI Wizard (Low priority)
            When a character crosses an XP threshold, a modal guides them
            through: +HP roll, ASI or feat choice, new class features,
            spell slot increases. Logic exists in lib/dnd5e/leveling.ts;
            the UI flow does not yet exist.

  FUTURE-4  Feats and Multiclassing (Low priority)
            Feat selection at ASI levels (every 4 levels for most classes).
            Multiclassing prerequisites and combined spell slot table.

  FUTURE-5  Visual Map / Encounter Builder (Low priority)
            Grid-based tactical map for combat encounters.
            The current text-only approach is considered fully playable;
            this is an enhancement for groups that prefer visual combat.

  FUTURE-6  Character Export (Low priority)
            Export character sheet as PDF or JSON for use outside
            the platform (printing, D&D Beyond import, etc.).

  FUTURE-7  Mobile Optimization (Low priority)
            The current layout is desktop-first. A responsive mobile layout
            would expand the addressable audience.

  FUTURE-8  Mana Bar (High prioroty)
            Make a mana bar right below health bar to show the player's
            character's mana (in current/total format) with a bar. It must be
            different depending on classes, for example, spellcaster's will
            have more mana and fighter's will have none.

8. TECHNICAL ARCHITECTURE SUMMARY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

8.1 Stack
  Frontend:         Next.js 16 (App Router), React 19, TypeScript 5
  Backend:          Railway/Next.js
  Styling:          Tailwind CSS 4
  State:            Zustand 5 (client), Supabase Realtime (server push)
  Database / Auth:  Supabase (PostgreSQL + Auth + Realtime)
  AI — Narration:   Groq API, Llama 3.3 70B
  AI — Summary:     Groq API, Llama 3.1 8B Instant
  AI — Copilot:     Anthropic API, Claude Haiku
  Deployment:       Vercel

8.2 Data Flow (AI Mode)
  1. Player submits action via ActionPanel
  2. POST /api/dm with campaign ID, character ID, action text
  3. Server fetches context from Supabase (campaign, chars, NPCs, quests,
     world memory summaries)
  4. systemPrompt.ts assembles the full DM system prompt
  5. Groq Llama 3.3 70B returns narration text with embedded XML events
  6. gameEvents.ts parses XML tags from the narration
  7. gameEventApplier.ts writes each event to Supabase
  8. Narration message is stored in messages table
  9. Supabase Realtime broadcasts the new message to all clients
  10. All clients update their Zustand store with the new message
      and any character stat changes

8.3 Database Tables
  users               — auth users mirror
  campaigns           — campaign records with settings and world state
  campaign_members    — user ↔ campaign junction
  characters          — full character data (JSONB for complex state)
  messages            — all narration, actions, dice rolls, combat log
  npcs                — persistent NPC records per campaign
  quests              — quest tracking per campaign
  world_memory_summaries — session summaries for long-term DM context
  combat_encounters   — active and historical combat state

8.4 API Routes
  POST /api/dm                    AI DM narration endpoint
  POST /api/dm-console/narrate    Human DM broadcast endpoint
  POST /api/dm-console/assist     AI copilot suggestion endpoint
  POST /api/campaign              Campaign creation
  POST /api/combat/start          Initiate combat encounter
  POST /api/combat/action         Resolve player combat turn
  POST /api/dice                  Generic dice roll

9. CONSTRAINTS AND DEPENDENCIES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  - Groq API availability and rate limits affect real-time narration latency.
  - Anthropic API availability affects the AI copilot and complex monster AI.
  - Supabase free tier limits on realtime connections and database size
    constrain the number of concurrent campaigns at scale.
  - D&D 5e SRD rules are used throughout; content outside the SRD (some
    classes, spells, monsters) is intentionally omitted to avoid IP concerns.
  - The platform is web-only. No mobile app is planned in the current scope.

10. OPEN QUESTIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  OQ-1  Monetization: How will API costs be covered at scale?
        Options: subscription, credits, free tier with limits.

  OQ-2  Content moderation: The AI DM can generate any content.
        Should there be guardrails for violent or adult content,
        and how should they be configured per campaign?
        The AI DM can generate anything, given that the user is over 18,
        and can generate violent scenes if the user is over 16. If UNDER 16,
        The DM must be very moderate in what it generates.

  OQ-3  Campaign length: Currently there is no mechanism to "end" a campaign
        beyond abandoning it. Should the AI DM be able to write a narrative
        conclusion, and should completed campaigns be archived?
        Yes. If a user says end campign, the AI DM must make a summary of the
        campaign so that the player can get a proper look at their campaign,
        then ask for confiormation and then archive the campaign.

  OQ-4  Solo play: The platform technically supports single-player campaigns
        (one player, AI DM) but this has not been explicitly tested or
        designed for. Is solo play a first-class use case?
        Yes

  OQ-5  Spell system scope: A full 5e spell system (900+ spells) is a major
        undertaking. Should the MVP spell system cover only the most common
        spells (top 30–50) rather than aiming for completeness?
        Aim for comprehensiveness.
